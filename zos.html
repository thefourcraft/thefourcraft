What Is A Zero Trust System?

Zero Trust is a strategic approach to cybersecurity that secures an organization by eliminating implicit trust and continuously validating every stage of digital interaction.

Zero Trust is designed to protect modern environments and enable digital transformation by using strong authentication methods, leveraging network segmentation, preventing lateral movement,
providing Layer 7 threat prevention, and simplifying granular, “least access” policies.

Like the old saying  “never trust, always verify”

Understanding Zero Trust Architecture

In Zero Trust, one of the first steps is the identification of the network’s most critical and valuable data, assets, applications, and services everything that you would call sensitive: dashboards, control panels, etc.

This helps prioritize where to start and also enables the creation of Zero Trust security policies.

By identifying the most critical assets, organizations can focus efforts on prioritizing and protecting those assets as part of their Zero Trust journey.

Making all the network assets zero trust doesn't make any sense, this is why step 0 is the most important one.

The next step is understanding who the users are, which applications they are using and how they are connecting to determine and enforce a policy that ensures secure access to your critical assets, in a zero trust system you can't give everyone access, policies will be based by each user group (developers, clients, workers).

Building The Zero Trust

Although Zero Trust is typically associated with securing users or use cases such as
Zero Trust Network Access (ZTNA).
A comprehensive zero trust approach encompasses Users, Applications, and Infrastructure.
If all is taken into account then your organization is on the right track for building a ZTNA

Users

Step one of any Zero Trust effort requires strong authentication of user identity, application of “least access” policies, and verification of user device integrity

Applications

Applying Zero Trust to applications removes implicit trust with various components of applications when they talk to each other.

A fundamental concept of Zero Trust is that applications cannot be trusted and continuous monitoring at runtime is necessary to validate their behavior.
3

Infrastructure

Everything infrastructure-related routers, switches, cloud, IoT,
and supply chain must be addressed with a Zero Trust approach
to ensure the highest level of security.
